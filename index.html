Agents are everywhere. How do we use them without giving up control?

Continuity

"Hey TV, find that video I was watching on my laptop"

Your Amazon agent needs browser history. Amazon shouldn't get to keep all of it.

Personalization

"Build my dating profile from my actual interests"

An agent could personalize any app in seconds — if you let it read your entire digital life.

Context

"Compare my sleep to my workout schedule this month"

Multi-source queries are powerful. Unrestricted data access is not the answer.

Why should their agent get raw access to your data?

Today, AI agents connect to your services directly — email, browser, health data — and you hope they only look at what they need. There's no middle ground between full access and no access.

Aperture creates that middle ground. Your own guardian agents sit between your data and every third party. They run locally, follow your rules, and share only what's needed. The requesting agent gets an answer — never raw access.

You set the rules

Grant full access to apps you trust. Let your Guardian Agent handle routine decisions autonomously. Require your explicit OK for sensitive categories. Every app, every data type — you choose the level of control.

Guardian Agents

Two agents that work for you, not for the requester. The Policy Guardian checks your rules with deterministic logic first. The Retrieval Guardian searches the vault and returns only what's needed. Third parties get answers — not raw data.

MCP Servers

CLI & API Providers

Wrap command-line tools and APIs as gated providers. If it runs on your machine, Aperture can control access to it and broker results through your guardians.
